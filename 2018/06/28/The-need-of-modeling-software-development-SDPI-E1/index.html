The need of modeling software development [SDPI-E1]

İbrahim Serdar's Blog
2018-06-28

The long title would be; The need of modeling software development and why it is so hard in 5 years long satellite projects.

Prologue

Nowadays software development is guided by open-source folks. This guidance had great impact on how a software developer think on his/her art. There are countless useful practices, tools and notions emerged in the last two decades. It would be painful work if I try to mention most of them here, so I will drop this subject here and continue to my main subject. But the reason I mentioned this; a lot of good practices are available from these folks but irl most of them can’t be applied directly in our particular domain. At least I couldn’t manage to apply them easily. I will try to convey my experiences about conducting a relatively long software project along with a agile mind.

Progression

When you find yourself in a project that lasts more than a couple of years, and you are great fan of agile development, you start to think most of the process steps of software development are unnecessary or done ineffectively. This idea is the beginning of the exhausting journey, but luckily there are few writings on this subject and I hope one of them could be this post series.

There are many studies indicate that long-term planning and acting is not proper for software development. Ye, I know that if I state this sentence I should give at least a couple references. So I can recommend you to read “The XP Programmer: The Few-Minutes Programmer” by Laurie Williams. The extreme programming methodologies encourages not only developers but also all stakeholders to collaborate each other. Communicating with all stakeholders makes the development process more efficient. This is no secret. Extreme programming elicits the requirements with user stories which written in natural language, customers also prioritize the requirements with a collective work. With this approach, the bitter question emerges: How can you occupy your stakeholders more than a couple of years? Moreover, this couple of years is just for requirements analysis and design phase. There will be implementation, testing-integration (we can tell acceptance) and deployment. All of these phases should made with a collaborative work, but is it possible? Superficial answer would be a “no”. But, mankind is insistent and too curious to be satisfied with this answer.

Conclusion

I hope with this post, you become convinced to dive into the idea of modeling software development process in my domain so that we can establish the framework of collaborative work and state-of-art software development processes. And also I hope you would agree with me on my process to define what is the state and what can be done. So, let this post be our prologue on this journey. I would like to continue my research with examining the work done by ECSS which named Agile Software Development Handbook.

See you on next posts.
