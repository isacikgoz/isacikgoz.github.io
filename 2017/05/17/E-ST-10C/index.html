What is ECSS?

First of all, let’s start with ECSS. It is abbreviation of European Cooperation for Space Standardization. As the name explicitly yells, it is an organization for space standards. As 2017 they have 4 groups of standards and they are: engineering, management, product assurance, and sustainability.

The Standard

In this post I will focus on ECSS-E-ST-10C Space Engineering - System engineering general requirements document.
Before reading a formal/serious standard, it is a good to being aware of the purpose of any standard is to improve the overall quality of the effort. tl;dr: To save money. In fact, there is a nice quote from a guy -I couldn’t remember his name but I think he can probably be my instructor- “The only valid measurement of the quality is money.” That means; quality is a indicator of what you buy for the money you gave. So, these standards aims to save labor-force, rework and other things so that large projects can fit those tight budgets. Also there is a risk factor, and I think reducing the risks is eventually converges to save money.

Opinions

I appreciate the effort for preparing these standards. They cover the domain with a structured way. The E-ST-10C defines system engineering function by it’s responsibilities, interactions and processes. I like the way they include space mission analysis into the system engineering. With this approach I think they build this document around the functionality rather than manageability . And this is, for me, a bold and splendid start.
There is also plenty od information about what is the elements of defined system engineering. As the nature of system engineering which is a complicated discipline, the E-ST-10C refers other ECSS standards. This makes the document a little challenging to read. To sum up, I think it is a good start to implement this system engineering approach to a space system project. It is a good fellow guide to carry in our pockets.
