ISO/IEC 12207 Software Life Cycle Processes

İbrahim Serdar's Blog
2017-10-09

In the late 80s where software is mostly done by ad-hoc methodologies. The lack of a uniform framework for managing and engineering for software development forced JTC1 to initiate the 12207 standard. The standard has same concepts to introduce. A top level architecture of the life cycle of software that visualizes the beginning and the end of software life-cycle process. The life cycle present the relationships between processes. A modularity concept for the processes that they are low coupled and high cohesive. A responsibility concept for quality management of set of software parties. The processes are divided into three major classes which primary supporting and organizational. And each process is further designed in terms of its own constituent activities and set of tasks for this activities. A task is a set of actions and it takes inputs and gives outputs. It is expressed in the form of self, declaration, requirement, recommendation or permissible action.

There is also a evolution concept for process and their sub elements. Each process implements quality management principles. Thus, each process is responsible for performing its activities including activities. The standard also provides an improvement process for managing, controlling and improving the established processes and their performance. The standard is based upon the principles of general system engineering and a link between software. For example, the fabrication concept is evaluated coding with this approach. And it also provides the minimum system context for software. With this vision of the standard. An organization or party is reponsible for actions in the processes. With this approach there are two applicability concepts to projects and organizations.

The ISO/IEC 12207 standard has a response to the rapidly evolving software engineering discipline. Therefore the activities and tasks are what to do do items. The standard does not defines any certification criteria. The standard covers the full life-cycle of software system and caters to diverse. To apply this standard to an organization there are few prerequisites which trained personnel, familiarity with the organization’s policies, familiarity with the projects environment and clear understanding of the standard.

So, we have a running organization and we also have plenty of projects, let’s say we were not aware of the standard. What should we do? Well, there are no normative rules for applying and tailoring the standard. The standard guides a set of stops to application on a project but some helpful guidance, however, may be found in the ISO/IEC 12207 Guidebook or other literature. These are role in the life cycle, organizations’ policies, system life cycle, development models, types of software, documentation, evaluations, project characteristics. The ISO/IEC 12207 standard has a general guidance an applying itself to an organization. It also has a relationship with ISO 9001 standard. These are at the process level, the ISO,IEC and JTC1 have been active in the standardization of quality assurance, process assessment, and life cycle processes. The former has been accomplishment at the system level; the latter two are being pursued at the software level. ISO/IEC 12207 provides the baselines of life cycle processes to the SPICE assessment process. It expected that as these standards evolve in the future, their mutual consistencies would improve.

To sum up this standard follows a well organized processes to implement quality in software development and management process. It takes its essence from other standards. With applying these processes into an organization or a project, the managing process would be easier. On the other hand there is a learning curve -which is a huge drawback- and tackling the resitance is challenging.
